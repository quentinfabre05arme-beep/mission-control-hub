# X Post Content Library

## Post Types by Category

### 🔷 TYPE A: Data Drop
**Structure:**
1. Lead number (surprising stat)
2. Context (what it means)
3. Implication (why it matters)
4. Forward look (what to watch)

**Example:**
> "ETH ETFs +$45M yesterday despite flat price.
> 
> Institutions accumulating without momentum.
> 
> When price breaks $2,000, supply squeeze risk rises.
> 
> Watch: 5 consecutive days above $50M inflows."

---

### 🔷 TYPE B: Educational Thread (Single Post)
**Structure:**
1. Hook (common misconception)
2. Framework (how it actually works)
3. Examples (real data)
4. Takeaway (what this means for investors)

**Example:**
> "Most think liquid staking is just yield.
> 
> It's actually collateral infrastructure.
> 
> $15B in LSTs backing DeFi loans.
> 
> The yield is nice. The financial primitive is transformative."

---

### 🔷 TYPE C: Contrarian Take
**Structure:**
1. Consensus view (what everyone thinks)
2. Contrarian angle (why it might be wrong)
3. Evidence (data supporting contrarian view)
4. Implication (what to do differently)

**Example:**
> "Everyone waiting for ETH ETF 'approval pop.'
> 
> Approval already happened. Flows are the signal now.
> 
> $1.2B in ETHA already. Quiet accumulation.
> 
> The move already started — most missed it."

---

### 🔷 TYPE D: Technical Levels
**Structure:**
1. Current situation
2. Key levels (support/resistance)
3. On-chain context
4. Bias + invalidation

**Example:**
> "ETH at $1,920 — decision point.
> 
> Support: $1,850 / $1,800 / $1,750
> Resistance: $2,000 / $2,100 / $2,200
> 
> Funding neutral, OI healthy.
> 
> Bias: Range-bound until ETF narrative reaccelerates.
> Invalidation: Daily close below $1,800."

---

### 🔷 TYPE E: Thesis/Evolution
**Structure:**
1. Current narrative (what's being discussed)
2. Deeper thesis (the real story)
3. Evidence (infrastructure, adoption, flows)
4. Implication (long-term positioning)

**Example:**
> "Obsession: ETH price.
> Reality: Ethereum infrastructure buildout.
> 
> $500M tokenized treasuries.
> 24/7 settlement vs T+2.
> Programmable collateral.
> 
> Infrastructure play > speculation play.
> 
> This is why institutions are accumulating."

---

### 🔷 TYPE F: Comparison/Frame
**Structure:**
1. Comparison setup (A vs B)
2. Key differences (data-driven)
3. Investment implication (which to prefer when)
4. Nuance (risks, exceptions)

**Example:**
> "ETH vs BTC in 2024:
> 
> BTC: ETF flows +$15B, 'digital gold' thesis
> ETH: ETF flows +$2B, 'infrastructure' thesis
> 
> BTC: Proven, institutional favorite
> ETH: Emerging, infrastructure optionality
> 
> Portfolio: Both. But ETH has more upside if DeFi scales."

---

## Hook Templates

### For Data Posts
- "[Number] thing everyone is missing about [topic]"
- "Just noticed [surprising data point]. Here's why it matters..."
- "[Number] million [asset] just moved. Here's what it means..."

### For Educational Posts
- "Most people think [common view]. Actually..."
- "Quick thread on how [complex topic] actually works:"
- "3 things I learned researching [topic]:"

### For Contrarian Posts
- "Unpopular opinion: [contrarian take]"
- "Everyone focused on [A]. The real story is [B]."
- "Hot take: [consensus view] is wrong. Here's why..."

### For Technical Posts
- "ETH at [price] — key levels to watch:"
- "Technical update: [asset] approaching [level]"
- "Support/resistance for [asset] this week:"

## Quality Checklist

Before posting:
- [ ] Data is current (within 24h for flows, 1h for price)
- [ ] Numbers are accurate (double-check sources)
- [ ] Hashtags relevant (not spam)
- [ ] Tone appropriate for topic
- [ ] Forward-looking element included
- [ ] Risk/invalidation mentioned (for market posts)
